About Diabetes

1. Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body processes blood sugar (glucose). There are two main types of diabetes: Type 1 and Type 2.
2.In Type 1 diabetes, the body's immune system attacks and destroys the cells in the pancreas that produce insulin, the hormone that regulates blood sugar levels. In Type 2 diabetes, the body either doesn't produce enough insulin or becomes resistant to its effects.
3.Diabetes can cause a range of health complications, including damage to the eyes, kidneys, and nerves, as well as an increased risk of heart disease and stroke. Management of diabetes typically involves a combination of medication, lifestyle changes, and regular monitoring of blood sugar levels.

We have 8 attributes that can be helpful in determining whether the person has diabetes.


  • Number of Pregnancies : Multiple pregnancies can cause changes in the body that affect insulin resistance, which increases the risk of developing Type 2 diabetes later in life, especially if the pregnancies resulted in gestational diabetes.

  • Glucose Level :Glucose is a type of sugar that is the body's primary source of energy. Glucose levels can be measured through a blood test, which can help determine if a person has diabetes,High glucose levels generally indicate diabetes, In general, a normal fasting blood glucose level is below 100 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L), and a level of 70 mg/dL (3.9 mmol/L) or higher is considered safe for most people.

  • Blood Pressure:High blood pressure is a risk factor for developing diabetes, but there is no specific blood pressure level that indicates the condition. A reading of 130/80 mmHg or higher may indicate high blood pressure and should be monitored closely by a healthcare provider.

  • Skin Thickness:Skin thickness is not a standard diagnostic test for diabetes, but it may be used as an indicator of insulin resistance, which is a risk factor for developing Type 2 diabetes. Thicker skin or higher levels of subcutaneous fat may be associated with insulin resistance and a higher risk of developing diabetes, but other factors should also be considered.

  • Insulin Level :Insulin is a hormone that regulates blood sugar levels. A normal fasting insulin level is 5-20 Î¼IU/mL, but can vary depending on the individual and the laboratory. High insulin levels may indicate insulin resistance, a risk factor for Type 2 diabetes. Low insulin levels may indicate Type 1 diabetes.

  • Body Mass Index:Body mass index (BMI) is a measurement of body fat based on height and weight. It is useful in determining diabetes because obesity is a risk factor for developing Type 2 diabetes. High BMI can indicate increased risk for developing diabetes.

  • Diabetes Pedigree Function :Diabetes pedigree function is a tool that uses family history to estimate an individual's risk of developing diabetes. A score of 0.15 or higher indicates increased risk.

  • Patient Age :Age is a key factor in determining diabetes risk, as the likelihood of developing the disease increases with age. Type 2 diabetes is more common in people over 45, while type 1 diabetes typically occurs in children and young adults.

  • Albumin:The kidneys play an important role in filtering albumin from the blood and preventing it from being lost in the urine. Therefore, the level of albumin in the urine can be an indicator of kidney function.The normal range for albumin in the urine, also known as the urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio (UACR), is less than 30 milligrams per gram of creatinine (mg/g). UACR values between 30-299 mg/g may indicate early kidney disease or increased risk for kidney disease, while values above 300 mg/g are generally considered a sign of advanced kidney disease.
